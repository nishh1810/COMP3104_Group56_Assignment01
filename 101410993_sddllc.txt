9The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a framework that defines a systematic process for developing software. It provides structure and guidelines for software development teams to follow, ensuring that the end product meets quality standards, fulfills business needs, and is delivered in a timely and cost-effective manner.The SDLC is a widely accepted methodology in the software industry, and it includes various stages that guide the development process from inception to completion.Understanding SDLC is crucial for both developers and stakeholders because it lays out the steps involved in transforming an idea into a functional software application.SDLC also plays a critical role in ensuring the quality of the software. Through detailed planning, design, and rigorous testing, it ensures that the final product is free of major bugs and meets both functional and non-functional requirements.
